Quietly, Quiggly stepped into the darkness as he stealthily approached and steeled himself for what was to come. He was alone and at the top of a tall staircase; the stone in the walls on the opposite side was cold and wet and slippery, and just to his left, a stairway descended toward him. To his right, in front of him, was a small landing that appeared to lead to a door. Quiggly was unsure of what was happening to him or where he was; however, after a moment, he realized he was in a basement. Quiggly wasn't sure what happened next. He believed that a hand came from behind him, gripping him by the shoulder. Suddenly, he found himself standing on his own two feet in front of a large blue car. He then heard a small voice say, "What do you think?" Quiggly looked around the car and saw a familiar brown figure standing by his side. "I need you to go to the front door and get the key and then drive the car," said the familiar brown figure. Quiggly didn't question it; he was just glad the hand was gone from around his shoulder.
