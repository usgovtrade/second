Friendly Fire” was created by the Israeli-American musician and artist Yoav. It is written in both Hebrew and English and explores the effects of war on life, including on people who have nothing to do with it. As Israel continues to expand its settlements in the West Bank, the Israeli government claims that these regions have no real meaning, meaning that their inhabitants are not human. (Image: Flickr/mikki gks Credit: ) “There are all types of reasons why wars happen. None of them are good,” Yoav wrote in an email. “War is terrible and it’s terrible for everybody—all civilians, no matter where they are from, are victims of it.” Yoav’s project is unique in that it reflects the artist’s own life. When he decided to write the song, he visited Israel and went on a tour of the West Bank with a Palestinian friend. It was on that tour that he “realized for the first time how complex the situation is.” I want to take care of the other … The last thing I want is war. Peace, what a concept. Peace be with you, bro. Peace, peace be with you. -Yoav “In a way, [the song] grew from my own trip to the West Bank and the Palestinian people, and from the conversation we had about the current situation in Israel,” Yoav wrote. “It goes with the spirit of what they’re doing, their struggle, their efforts to try and find a way to live. Even the Arabic part in the song represents that struggle. I really tried to reach deep into that conflict and touch something very much in there—especially when I write in the other language of the song, Hebrew, and when I tried to go into the meaning of words I haven’t used in a long time, to their essence.” When the song was officially released, Yoav wrote that he was “struck” with the reactions to it, with some loving it and others hating it. “It touched people and it touched them in a deep way,” he wrote, “because it shows that for me, and for a lot of people that I know, we really care about human beings.” Yoav has performed “Friendly Fire” at his shows for nearly five years now and says that each time he sings it, it touches people in a deeper way. “And I hope that through this song, it will show you what is going on out there in the world, the world that we live in, and how we can do better.” He added that he writes many of his songs about injustice and wants people to be conscious of this “war with ourselves” that is going on, and to “try to find our better part.” Yoav hopes that people will consider his efforts to show the reality of the conflict. As the Israeli military claims that the settlements in the West Bank have no real meaning, Yoav is hoping to create music that is meaningful and relevant to both sides.
