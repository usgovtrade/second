A man with dementia is living on the streets of Philadelphia. Because of the warm weather, a new disease, scurvy, has taken its toll on his health. As a result, his feet are incredibly swollen. When he arrives at the ER, the staff is not sure if he is suffering from a bacterial infection, a viral infection, or scurvy. The physicians have to put their heads together to figure out what is wrong with the man. They finally discover that he is suffering from all three illnesses, and he will be just fine with the right treatments. They decide to place him on antibiotics for the bacterial infection, vitamin C therapy for the scurvy, and anti-inflammatory medications to control the swelling in his feet. As a result, the man has surgery for the feet, and he is back on his feet walking around town before the day is over. The doctors discover that there are other cases like the man's in the ER. It's all about communication and a team approach to solving the medical problems. A lesson learned is: There are a lot of medications out there that can cure many different illnesses, but only if you can figure out what is going on and treat the correct illness.
