Didn’t they tell you that I was a ḥaẓẓot?” R. Joshua b. Levi used to say: “He’s ḥaẓẓot of all sorts of things; when I told him to open [the door of the bet midrash] in the winter, he said: ‘I won’t open the door except in the summer.’ When they told him to open in the summer, he said: ‘I won’t open in the summer unless it is raining.’” R. Ḥiyya bar Ashi said: “He’s the chief of the wicked among all the people of the world, and he is their ‘dove’.” According to the Sages, he is so called in relation to his being a ḥaẓẓot and because he takes the place of the ḥaẓẓot and acts as an interpreter.
