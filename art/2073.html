The Importance of Being Eldest_ was written while she was convalescing from her stroke. She had a stroke at the age of 63; a few years later she developed dementia. When she was in her fifties, she started writing a novel called _The Secret Adversary,_ inspired by a mystery novel she had read as a child. The original manuscript was heavily edited and re-edited by Bester and he became dissatisfied with the end result. When he died, he instructed that it should be published with all his annotations included, but since his death, several of these annotators have disputed the veracity of some of the annotations. The book was finally published by Ace Books in 1977; her version was not approved until 1980. She began the novel in 1953, as a response to being the oldest and most junior of seven children, by writing a novel in which her family members have different nationalities, including one who is from the moon. The novel follows the story of her family after the grandmother's death, a journey that took her into Spain. Mostly, she traveled to Europe and developed a penchant for European cities, architecture, cafés, streets, and small, intimate restaurants. Although the novel has a mystery that is solved, as in Agatha Christie novels, the protagonist is a modern Cinderella and her brothers (except for the hero) are not princes. (Although it may seem unlikely, in her lifetime, she was never the "eldest" of her siblings.) The plot of the novel is intricate and there are many twists and turns, but eventually the identity of her great-great-great-uncle is discovered. This man was the architect of the family fortune. After leaving the U.S. _—Lonelyhearts_ was a failure in England, but it took off in France and she began writing French novelettes—she lived in Paris for five years and began to write in French. During the 1950s, she spent time in Paris, living in an apartment that overlooked the Arc de Triomphe. She then went to live in Rome, Italy, and rented a suite of rooms in an apartment in a 19th-century building where she also had a maid who did her hair. As an author, she had a high standing in Rome and developed a large correspondence with readers. Her neighbors included two other novelists, an opera singer, and a painter who also lived on the top floor of the apartment building. Bester and the woman who rented the lower two floors were her friends for many years. She eventually had a stroke and lost the use of her right side. It took several years of therapy to relearn many things and regain her abilities, including a driver's license, which she had not had for decades. She lived in Paris during World War II with her first husband, a Greek archaeologist. Their marriage ended in divorce. She met and married her second husband, a man named Hymen Bull, in Rome in 1951. Their marriage lasted seven years. He was an adventurer and a man of many interests, including flying and writing adventure novels. They lived in Paris, Rome, Bermuda, and Hawaii. They had no children. The novels she wrote after _The Magic of Reality_ are not always considered by critics to be as strong as the first novel. The next six novels were written when she was in her eighties: _The Age of the Pussyfoot_ (1975), _The Children's Hour_ (1976), _The Adoration of Jenna Fox_ (1976), _The Compleat Algolian_ (1978), _Jad-Es-Rakim_ (1978), and _No Good from a Corpse_ (1979). Each of her novels in her later years had a central female character, and her novels all contain strong mysteries, but the characters vary in complexity. She was most successful with strong female characters. She continued to write short stories and one more novel, _The Alaskan Story: Or, The Exquisite Corpse,_ in 1980. _The Secret Adversary_ is as much a mystery as a romance, and the character of Mrs. Imber, a formidable spinster, the matriarch of the family, dominates the book. The _Alaskan Story_ may also be considered a mystery in the vein of Agatha Christie. It is not a mystery, however, in the typical sense. It is a science fiction story. Her last novel has a strong supernatural theme, but is not supernatural in the manner that some supernatural novels are. There are also elements of comedy and adventure in the story, as there are in her earlier novels, but the overall feeling is that this is an adventure novel. Her short stories have continued to appear in anthologies and collections. She continued to travel and she gave public readings. The _Alaskan Story_ is an autobiographical book in which she also wrote about the importance of the written word. She and the second husband divorced in 1984. Her last novel was _The Adoration of Jenna Fox,_ published in 1978. The last short story collection, _The Best of Judith Merril,_ was published posthumously.
