The IDA is funded by state tax credits and donations from corporations and foundations. Any donation to the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity through the IDA is eligible for a 65% tax credit against federal income taxes, thus, helping provide jobs and economic growth for Illinois. To date, the IDA has helped fund almost 1,000 business projects across the state creating over 51,000 jobs and over $13 billion in investment. In Chicago alone, the IDA has helped fund over 1,600 projects creating almost 65,000 jobs and $13.2 billion in investment. The programs continue to support new businesses and development in both urban and rural areas throughout the state. The IWTA was established by Public Act 94-1008, known as The Job Training Investment Act. The purpose of this act was to create a competitive grant program for workforce investment that provides training grants to companies who create or retain jobs in Illinois. "This great group of organizations understand that we can't grow our economy without putting people to work," said Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity (DCEO) Director Sean McCarthy. "Our state's diverse business climate will only continue to flourish if we can educate and train a skilled workforce. Our partners support our efforts through investments in programs that have helped place hundreds of thousands of unemployed Illinoisans back into the workforce and helped hundreds of companies keep their employees current and expand into new markets." About the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity The mission of the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity (DCEO) is to develop an efficient, customer-friendly state government that is good for citizens and business alike. Our goals are smart economic growth for all communities across the state; a world-class workforce that is educated, skilled, trained, certified, credentialed and ready for the jobs of tomorrow; access to capital; a modern infrastructure that meets the needs of business today and tomorrow; environmental responsibility; energy security; and a healthful environment for all. We strive to make government a good partner to business and are dedicated to serving as a one-stop shop for Illinois companies to identify funding, capital, resources and experts available to help their business succeed. About A Better Chance of Illinois The mission of the A Better Chance of Illinois (ABC) is to eliminate poverty, unemployment and welfare dependency for minorities in our society through education. Founded in 1973 by an elite group of prominent African American professionals from the Chicagoland area, ABC's early successes have resulted in thousands of disadvantaged youth and young adults participating in their nationally recognized program. Under the tutelage of some of America's most highly educated individuals, ABC of Illinois students are prepared to compete successfully for admission to the nation's top colleges and universities, thus breaking the cycle of low educational attainment that plagues minority communities. In addition to their educational successes, ABC alumni have become leading professionals, entrepreneurs and community leaders. For more information about ABC of Illinois please visit www.abchicago.org or call 773-636-9366.
