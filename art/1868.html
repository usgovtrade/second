These data will be used to improve the efficiency of machine learning. What is Machine Learning? The development of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning will be the next revolution in computing, affecting every aspect of life. At its simplest, Machine Learning is when an algorithm or computer is given some data and then figures out ways to use it. For example, it could analyse all the messages we send on Twitter and figure out how people connect with each other. Or it could identify a person in a photo on Instagram, automatically and by themselves. Machine Learning could be used by self-driving cars to plan ahead – which lane to drive into, which turn to take, which direction to go in when there are different options. The cars could also learn to avoid traffic jams, bad weather and road closures by themselves. The algorithms would draw on many different types of information, such as GPS location, traffic reports, and data from other cars. They might also take into account information about the weather or the number of people that are likely to be out on a particular day. When you think about it, humans learn like this too. We learn to talk because we receive information about our environment from the sounds of the world around us. If a parent is encouraging a child to talk, then they are providing information about the sounds they want to hear. Other machine learning applications could include the following: · Better healthcare – Artificial Intelligence and machine learning could improve the way medical decisions are made and help with diagnostics. This could save lives and give people greater freedom to be independent. · Improved home care – Self-driving cars could allow the elderly to continue to live independently and still get help if needed.
