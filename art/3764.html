She was also one of the contestants on The Mole, a British reality show in the style of The Weakest Link, on which she was known for her strong views on a wide variety of subjects and her loud and colourful dress sense. Later career After splitting from her husband in 2005, and after much work to come to terms with her sexuality, she had developed an interest in singing and dancing. After having gained a lot of interest from the media through The Mole, she came back into the public eye with more of her own music, mainly being soul and R&B infused, but has continued to tour her cabaret shows throughout the UK. She was a regular on The Last Leg on Channel 4. In 2007, she filmed a solo sex comedy for television called, Sex Drive. Sex Drive is a TV show made by a film company about a London PR who gets a driving licence. In 2008, she started touring with a brand new show "Dame Edna: The Female Impersonator" in the UK and Ireland. This was her first ever all female show. She performed this show at Edinburgh Festival, Brighton, London, The Palace Theatre, Oxford and Wembley Arena, amongst others. Leah returned to The Mole in September 2009 as one of the 11 finalists. Leah starred in Channel 4's All Star Mr & Mrs. In 2011, she performed with Rufus Hound on his BBC Radio 2 Saturday morning show Houndswipe. In 2013, Leah returned to The Mole alongside Simon Day and Tom Allen. In May 2014 Leah performed as part of the BBC's Civilisations project. The event also featured Professor Brian Cox, and was filmed in New York. Leah starred in BBC's Strictly Come Dancing. In October 2015, Leah went on tour in Ireland with her show "Dame Edna: The Female Impersonator". In January 2016, she released a comedy special titled 'Facts are Innit'. In July 2016, Leah was part of Channel 4's '100 Greatest Christmas Moments'. She was also the narrator for the 2015 special of EastEnders with Dame Barbara Windsor. Leah provided the voice over for the new BBC series The Voice and the spin off show The Voice: the Blind Auditions. Leah is currently performing in a UK tour of A Night at the Musicals alongside singer Jamie Campbell and comedian John Hodgkinson. The shows have been performed around the UK, including Leeds, and have been positively received.
