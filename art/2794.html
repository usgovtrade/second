On October 11, 2016, Kanye West posted an image of himself on his website, Kanyeuniverse.com, in which he was wearing brown contacts. The next day, the "All Day" producer said that the glasses were a result of him going blind in one eye for a week following a vitreous hemorrhage in that eye. "I got into this new style of contacts where it's a gradient from green to dark, I think it's called the Masc-Emmers," he explained in an Instagram video, and added that he tried to get them approved for him to wear at the Grammys, but didn't "have the strength to beg." "I'm like so blind. I'm just so blind. It's so crazy. I can barely see," he said. This wasn't the first time he'd gone blind or worn strange glasses. In 2005, he was diagnosed with Bell's Palsy, which causes facial paralysis, when doctors said it was "definitely not from drinkin' too much Hennessy," as West famously told the radio station Hot 97. West had already tried to get past the disability when he wore "my little sunglasses" at the Grammy Awards, and explained that he's "never had issues with my eyes, but I got to my mom's house and I put some cool glasses on and one of the contacts had fallen out." He even went as far as having a specialist check his eyes before he left for Japan to meet the Koi Company, who "fixed" him, and he used the same glasses on stage at the Grammys. On Twitter, he revealed that the glasses are fake contacts he likes and had made himself. "I tried to put some contacts in but you can see I am an amateur," he wrote. "Fake eyes." His account seemed to mock the idea that his sunglasses were intended to be a signal of his distaste for the Grammy Awards — his original tweet about going blind after taking off the sunglasses was captioned, "I'm blind, bitch." — and he said as much himself. "They're not contacts, that's not what I was trying to do," he told GQ. "It's like me going, 'I know this thing has been crazy and everyone is losing their shit,' and I just wanted to show that I'm as blind as I wanna be," he said. "I’m just blind, it's real. It's my true self. The glasses are just more of a fashion." However, it would seem that West can't leave his sunglasses in the bag forever. In May, he confirmed that his eye is "still very cloudy and blurry," meaning that he hasn't worn the glasses in public. "They're for on-stage and stuff like that," he told TMZ, before adding that he still doesn't want to wear contacts. "I've gotten to the point where I know when I put contacts in, they're gonna crack my eyes. And I don't like wearing contacts." The glasses might be back on for the 2018 Grammy Awards.
