What You Should Expect from a Professional Wedding Planner There are a few things to consider if you hire a wedding planner. The main one is what kind of cost and experience you will get. The most important thing a wedding planner will do is advise you and make suggestions on how to make your wedding one to remember. He or she will check your plans to make sure everything works, provide an idea of what you can expect your wedding planner to do and be available to answer any questions you may have. Wedding planners are extremely knowledgeable about the venues available and the services offered by each one. They will know what are the best times to book a certain location. They will also know about possible discounts and will have connections with vendors who can get you a lower price on your decorations, flowers and music. They may also have connections with local churches, which can offer you a discount on the officiant's services. The average fee for a wedding planner is $25-$125 per hour. That may include several hours or a few hours for planning your wedding. Some wedding planners also charge a per-diem for meals. What Types of Wedding Planning Experience is Available? Each wedding planner has a unique style of doing business. Some planners are very particular about what style of wedding they will work with. This means the style should match their personal tastes. If you want to have a very simple wedding, your wedding planner may not be a good match. There are many things to consider when finding the perfect wedding planner. The first thing to consider is how much input you are able to give them. Do you want to work with a planner who does things your way or do you want to have a more custom wedding? Are you able to give your planner any ideas you have about colors, flowers, music and decorations? Are you looking for a wedding planner to walk you through the process from start to finish or are you happy with just having a point person in the plan? If you need help picking the right wedding planner, consider the following questions when interviewing them. What style of wedding do you prefer? Are you looking for a planner who will have input or will it be more traditional? What is your fee structure? Where did you get your license to plan weddings? How long have you been doing this? Do you work alone or are you part of a company? How many weddings will you plan in a year? Does your company have a special event coordinator who does the job alone, or are there multiple planners in your company? Are your other planners in your company also experienced in this area of wedding planning? Are you planning a wedding for a family member, a friend or a close acquaintance? Does your planner have experience planning your type of wedding?
