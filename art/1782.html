The most important consideration when choosing an 8-inch tablet is what it’s made of. Both the iPad Mini and Acer Liquid X2 5 have a very similar design, which isn’t surprising considering they are both from Taiwanese manufacturers. However, you’ll notice that the iPad Mini has a plastic back and a metal frame whereas the Liquid X2 5 has a polycarbonate back and metal frame. You’re probably wondering why a plastic back is bad when it seems to fit better with the iPad Mini. You’re also probably wondering why metal would be better, but you’re right to ask these questions. Most cheap 8-inch tablets are made of plastic which tends to bend, and this is exactly what happened to me. My iPad Mini actually bent. One time, when I picked it up with one hand, the frame bent and I had to get a paperweight out. It was terrible. The iPad Mini, on the other hand, has a much stronger build. When it comes to the Acer Liquid X2 5, you won’t have this problem since it’s so made of plastic and you’ll definitely need a plastic back to avoid such a problem. As for the Samsung Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1, you’ll need a plastic back too. It’s cheap, after all, so you should go for the Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1. Acer Liquid X2 5 vs Samsung Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1 The biggest difference is in the processor. Samsung Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1 has an A6 quad-core processor whereas the Acer Liquid X2 5 only has a dual-core processor. This could be a major factor in choosing which tablet to get. Both tablets have the same resolution, but the Samsung Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1 has 1280 x 800 pixels, while the Acer Liquid X2 5 only has 1024 x 768 pixels. The Acer Liquid X2 5 is available for $170 and it’s also more expensive than the Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1 at $230. Samsung Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1 vs iPad Mini As previously mentioned, the Acer Liquid X2 5 is similar to the iPad Mini in terms of design. However, the Samsung Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1 is the cheaper of the two. It’s around a third cheaper than the iPad Mini. Although it costs almost as much as the X2 5, it does come with twice the RAM. It also costs $100 more than the Galaxy Tab Pro 10.1.
