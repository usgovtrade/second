Each participant in the study was asked to undergo four simple medical tests — waist circumference, fasting blood glucose, body mass index and a blood test for C-reactive protein (CRP), which serves as an indicator of inflammation. The results showed that middle-age CRP levels correlated with average C-reactive protein concentrations in old age, but men who were overweight or obese in midlife and had high C-reactive protein levels were more likely to develop rheumatoid arthritis decades later than men with normal CRP levels.
