Museum of the Jewish People – is a huge complex with exhibition halls, an extensive library and even an audio-visual room dedicated to the Holocaust. The museum also offers a selection of guided tours. Jerusalem Archaeological Park – is where you can find the City of David, which sits above the Western Wall. The park has some excellent archaeological excavations that you should definitely explore while in Israel. The Western Wall, or Kotel, is one of the most significant places in Judaism. This wall, a remnant from the Second Temple period is today revered as a place of prayer. The Garden Tomb is another significant religious site in Jerusalem. For those interested in visiting this place, you can join a tour with Via Dolorosa who are based in Jerusalem. The Tower of David Museum – is dedicated to Jerusalem’s history and it’s full of artefacts and exhibitions highlighting different periods of its history. Jaffa Port is one of Israel’s oldest ports, which now stands as a working port. You can check out the excavated Roman harbour, watch a cruise ship leave the port or even buy some fresh fish from the market stalls. Mitzpe Ramon is an interesting town surrounded by stunning natural beauty. You can take a drive through one of the most accessible national parks in Israel, find out more about Mitzpe Ramon’s fascinating geology or head over to the nearby caves of Eshel Ben Ezra. Hammamat-Ghanem is an ancient village with striking Crusader-era architecture. Some of the most important archaeological discoveries made in Israel were found here in a Crusader Castle and an Ottoman Villa.
