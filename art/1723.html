Verified Reviewer Goodreads Author This is the second book in the Underwater Life series, and it is probably my favorite so far. Richard Ellis continues to tell an incredible story about a world that is so unfamiliar to me (i’m more of a land-based kind of person) and fills me with such wonder. The novel opens in the middle of a story that we’ve seen hints of, and we get to see all the threads that you were promised, slowly unravel. I love that Ellis does more with the underwater life than simply showing us the world below the sea. He goes as far as allowing us to meet some species, and shows us more details of their habitat. He shows the reader more than what is possible by using the perspective of the narrator to tell the story. We are taken on a wonderful ride as the story progresses from one mystery to another, leaving you guessing at the plot twists and turns before they happen. I love his writing style. There are very few books written that have the same effect on me as this series has on me. I can’t wait to read the final book. 5 out of 5 Elizabeth L. Verified Reviewer Goodreads Author It’s an understatement to say that this is the most epic underwater trilogy I’ve ever read, but in this stunning follow-up to The Blue I am not sure I can put a new label on it. The story opens with the mysterious disappearance of two submarines in a remote part of the ocean. And yes, our narrator who has a keen sense of being lost in life, is back for book 3. We are treated to the history of the submarines and the background story of life below the waves. Ellis’ ability to create an underworld setting as rich as any we’ve seen before has been phenomenal. If you’ve been wanting more and you don’t read the first two books in the series, don’t skip this entry. While the first book was mostly a love story, this one will entertain you whether you love submarine stories or are completely new to the genre. 5 out of 5 Molly L.
