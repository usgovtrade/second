We all know that water is the most important fluid for living creatures. But to fully understand this issue and our obligation to protect water quality, this article will offer some details about a special element: oxygen. Oxygen is required to maintain life. It’s so important, in fact, that if there is not enough oxygen in the air or in our water, we die. Even in our well-treated drinking water there is some oxygen that can be consumed by microorganisms. The quality of water is also negatively impacted by a number of additional compounds. These substances reduce the level of oxygen and can cause toxic effects: they are called contaminants. Types of Water Groundwater Groundwater is pure water that is contained in underground reservoirs which are called aquifers. Groundwater is stored in many different layers of rock and soil. In fact, it’s estimated that groundwater exists in about one-third of the earth’s surface. Groundwater is an important source of fresh water because it’s virtually untouched by other water sources. At the same time, groundwater is very vulnerable to pollution. That’s why groundwater has the largest surface area of pollution in the world. Groundwater pollution is the greatest threat to the environment because we cannot see it and we have no way to fully protect it. Its main sources are the following: Industrial contaminants and pollution from domestic households Agricultural practices Wastewater discharges from wastewater treatment plants Pollution of rivers and lakes that affect groundwater In order to protect our groundwater, countries have a set of regulations that are enforced by environmental officials. There are also many organizations and nongovernmental organizations that are trying to raise awareness about the importance of drinking water. Sea Water There is a huge difference between sea water and groundwater. Water in the sea is called marine water, and it’s a mixture of ocean water and fresh water. Although it sounds like there’s nothing special about this kind of water, in fact it’s quite difficult to find. In most cases, it’s very salty and full of minerals. The problem is that freshwater in the ocean is used for drinking and is called brackish water. Of course, water that is used for drinking, is also used by other people. This means that some water that has been used by humans can have high levels of chemicals and pathogens. In order to preserve drinking water from the sea, we use filtering systems, solar panels and other technologies. It’s not always enough though. Marine water is also being polluted by human waste from sanitation systems. Rainwater The third type of water is rainwater. Rainwater doesn’t enter our body via the pores of our skin because it is very diluted. Instead, rainwater enters our body through our respiratory system. Because it’s a naturally occurring substance, rainwater contains many minerals and gases that can’t be filtered out. When it comes to our drinking water, rainwater can become a problem because it’s hard to separate rainwater from contaminated sources of rainwater. Of course, we can collect rainwater in rain barrels or cisterns, but this will still increase our exposure to chemicals and bacteria. The good news is that rainwater is very alkaline. This means that it can neutralize all acids and reduce the risk of acidosis and ulcers in the stomach.
