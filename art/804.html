Chapter 1. Our story begins with the creation of the universe, which occurred approximately 14 billion years ago. I know that’s a pretty huge number, so how can we make heads or tails of it? I’m going to give you an example. I’ve never been to Disneyland, but I imagine they have a very well-appointed park. If you had told me that you lived in Disneyland and had been there for 14 billion years, I’d be totally lost. I’d have no frame of reference. It would be something like asking a native Australian to tell you about the dinosaurs, then to tell you about how life first began in our part of the universe, and then to tell you what life was like in the beginning of time. All of these things are equally foreign to us. I think it’s almost impossible to understand the beginning of time. However, if I take you back even further to the Big Bang, the Big Bang itself is part of a much smaller universe, and the universe has been expanding and cooling since the Big Bang. It’s that simple. If you took us all the way back to the beginning, we’d be back to that first instant of creation, where there was literally nothing in space.
