The present invention relates generally to the field of data entry systems, and more particularly, to automated methods and systems for verifying data of a type that is not directly discernible to the human visual system. In the field of inputting data for text processing, such as word processing and the like, machines for entering data and instructions often include keyboards and key pads. The keys and keypads are generally connected to electronic circuitry that controls the operations of a data processing apparatus. In operation, data may be inputted by manual manipulation of the keys or keypads. For example, data may be inputted into a word processing system by touching the tip of a pen to a button, and then depressing the button with the pen as the pen is placed against the page being written on. To enter data into a graphic tablet, similar manipulations are made on keys, which are provided on a board positioned within the tablet. The keys are connected to the electronic circuitry within the tablet that controls inputting of data into the tablet. To input data, the tip of the pen or a separate stylus is placed on a position on the tablet corresponding to the position of a button on the keyboard. A signal is sent to the processor, to instruct the processor to detect the position of the pen, and then input the data in the position on the tablet corresponding to the position of the pen on the keyboard. To control the operation of the tablet, the user generally must have some knowledge about what functions the particular tablet provides. In order to operate a tablet, a user must also have some knowledge about which button on the keyboard corresponds to each of the keys on the tablet. With a tablet, a data processing apparatus is difficult to learn how to use without first taking an initial tutorial course. Even with a user that is familiar with the operation of a tablet, an individual user will generally have difficulty in using a tablet in conjunction with a word processing system because of the need to familiarize oneself with the layout of a keyboard or keypad on a tablet. As such, tablet devices have limited use in any computer-related application. However, use of a tablet device with word processing or other applications that involve the input of data into a computer, would provide significant benefits to users, particularly those users who may be unfamiliar with operating keyboards and keypads. The use of a tablet device would also provide significant benefits to users that may not be adept at reading the information on a computer screen. For example, those users with only partial vision, or that are not adept at reading using the eyes, would find tablet devices beneficial to their efficient use of computers. Furthermore, tablet devices offer capabilities that are substantially different from the capabilities of keyboards. The present invention is a system and method for entering data into a computer using a data entry device that is different than a keyboard or keypad. Because a person is generally accustomed to inputting information in one form, such as by using a keyboard or keypad, the present invention offers a more intuitive user interface. A tablet device may be used with computer applications such as word processing, in which the user is provided with a screen to type on, and a tablet on which to make writing or drawing inputs. A stylus may be used to press buttons or otherwise input data into the computer. The stylus may also be used as a writing tool to input information onto a form that may be connected to the tablet. While a stylus is primarily used for writing on a screen and is not necessarily used to input data into a computer, the present invention allows for writing and drawing inputs to be used for data input as well. In the present invention, a pen is generally used with a form that has a plurality of areas on which to write, and/or a plurality of areas on which to make other inputs, such as drawing inputs. The areas on which data may be written generally comprise text, but are not limited to any one form of data. One or more areas on the form may be used for writing data, and a second area on the form may be used to write a title for the area that is being written on.
