Read our blog on: You are here: International Day of the Girl Child Today From a global perspective, the world has achieved incredible progress in advancing the rights, status, and health of girls and women. This year’s International Day of the Girl Child celebrates the first time we are at a meeting of the UN General Assembly to discuss the situation of girls and women. The meeting marks an important milestone for ending female genital mutilation (FGM) and forced marriage, two human rights abuses that, despite their global prevalence, have been insufficiently highlighted, understood, and tackled. FGM affects over 200 million girls and women globally—with more than 3 million girls estimated to undergo the practice each year. It is also a reminder that we have much more to do to promote gender equality.
