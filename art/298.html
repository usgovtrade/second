The outdoor cooking industry has responded to this demand for more and more features and made it easier to grill and smoke meat and fish with less fat and more flavor. In fact, while I am a proponent of traditional outdoor cooking over an open fire, one thing you will not find here is a traditional recipe for any one of these outdoor cookers. The world of outdoor cooking products has become an increasingly unique and diverse place over the past several years. I will say that this book can also be enjoyed by people who are just wanting a backyard cooker to get them started with smoking meat. Grill Grates are a great product that will expand what you can cook. I have friends who can't build a charcoal grill in their backyards and have an electric grill. Grill Grates allow them to smoke meats with a minimal amount of effort and create great smoke flavor. Justin Spicer, who is the designer and creator of Grill Grates, also believes in this very ethos. So while Grill Grates are a great product, I am surprised that the first book by him and his company covers just the Grill Grate. It seems that this is just the beginning of a huge line of outdoor cooking products and a wide variety of smokers for those who are interested in turning their backyard or deck into a professional-grade smoker. I can't wait to see how he expands this great product line to fit a variety of grilling needs. And that's just one reason why I am so excited about this new cookbook. It provides the detailed information you need to get started with the Grill Grate in a way that you will be able to enjoy an outdoor experience, including eating the fruits of your labor, that is better than you thought was possible. —C. "Chuck" Sonny Callahan, founder and executive editor of The BBQ Guy.com # Introduction: A Grilling Odyssey—Beginning with Grill Grates I am writing this book from the heart of the great outdoors—at my family's summer home on Lake Erie. In the evening, the skies can be filled with the sound of thunder as it rolls over this magnificent lake. When the skies clear, the stars seem to shine bright above me and the moon is a silver orb that gives warmth to the landscape. When the moon is full, you can see it from horizon to horizon. The stars shine against a backdrop of trees on the bluff to my left and the night sky above that. I have a panoramic view of the moon's reflection on the lake and over the water to the trees along the opposite shoreline. This place has everything: woods, mountains, ponds, and lake, all surrounded by a huge forest of oaks. You can find plenty of wildlife and many places to be by a blaze on a grate. There is not much I have done in life that compares to the thrill of spending time outdoors. I love to come out of my office and go fishing for crappie in the late spring and early summer. I enjoy casting a light spinning rod in early season and later in the season taking a heavy spinning rod and catching huge bass. I am also a master at ice fishing for pike and perch, and I enjoy the challenge of pulling them in. I enjoy being in the outdoors as much as I can; it's my life. I have been a professional barbecue chef since 2003, and I started smoking in 2003. I enjoy cooking and smoking my meats, and I love spending time outside in the great outdoors. The more barbecue joints I get to see, the more I enjoy it. But it's not just my life, it is my brother's, and it's his friends. We are always talking about food and outdoor cooking. We grill all the time at his house—almost every weekend we have dinner. I grill and smoke my own meat and fish and love to get more people to understand the excitement of smoking meat and grilling and the flavor it gives. I am not just into grilling meats and fish and other foods, I am into smoking. Grilling and smoking is such an art. The grills are made of all kinds of materials—wood, charcoal, gas, or electric. There is all kinds of fuel that you can use—charcoal, propane, wood, natural gas, and electricity. The materials for grills are endless and you can use them to take your grilling, smoking, and outdoor cooking to another level. This book is all about grilling and smoking and sharing the tools I have used to find what is right for me, and I hope you get the same enjoyment from the grill as I do. —J.
