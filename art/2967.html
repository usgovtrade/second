Just got back from the Riot Fest downtown, but haven't had time to put my thoughts together. I'll give it to you now, while it's still fresh in my mind. This year there were over 200 punk, metal, and alternative rock bands on three days of music all on one stage. This was only the second year for the fest to be held in Chicago, and it was an incredible experience. It took me two years to finally decide that I'd really like to go to Riot Fest, and I'm glad I made the trip. I came home with a couple of new records, but I took home the most fantastic memories. There were lots of big time bands like Rise Against and NOFX, but I went with a more nostalgic vibe of old friends and memories. The music was fantastic, the people were friendly, and it was just a very punk rock vibe going on. There was a lot of new punk on the bill, like CSTVT and Stray from the Path, but the music didn't match the vibe. The older bands had the best sets by far, and I walked away with some great memories. So much great punk music was on tap, and it just felt right to be at Riot Fest. I've seen a lot of punk bands come and go, and there are some that I've had a spot on the schedule for more than ten years. It was nice to be back with all the best bands of my youth, and great memories. Some of my favorite music that played at Riot Fest this year was: Bad Religion, Descendents, Face to Face, the Mighty Mighty Bosstones, Misfits, NOFX, Punk Rock Bowling, the Queers, Stray from the Path, Samiam, Screeching Weasel, the Casualties, the Vandals, X, Gwar, and The Exploited. I'm not really a fan of Rise Against, but they were on the bill and I saw them. It was an all out punk fest, and I was just glad I finally made it. Chicago always kicks ass.
