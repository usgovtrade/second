The invention relates generally to devices for storing, mounting, and aligning accessories for archery bows. Bow hunters are well aware of the dangers encountered when not maintaining a bow in a secure, safe, and accessible position. Bow hunters have experienced the frustration of a downed bow lying in the bushes or on the ground while they are chasing or tracking game. As a consequence of their misfortune, they have risked exposure to animal, weather, terrain, and other environmental conditions which could severely limit their opportunities for an adequate harvest. Those who are serious about the sport often consider the purchase of a bow carry and recovery device to be an important investment in their ability to obtain maximum results from hunting. Unfortunately, devices available for this purpose are not always reliable and sometimes are difficult to use. In order to minimize the inconvenience of such problems, the bow hunter must be diligent in finding, testing, and practicing use of the bow carry and recovery device before he or she is ready to hunt. It would be highly advantageous, therefore, to remedy the foregoing and other deficiencies inherent in the prior art. Accordingly, it is an object the present invention to provide a bow recovery device which will support the downed bow so that the archer can conveniently and effectively recover the bow for continuing his or her hunt. Another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device which is adjustable to enable mounting in a position most convenient for the archer. And another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a storage compartment and removable stabilizing member mounted in conjunction with a supporting bracket to maintain the bow in position. Still another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a storage compartment which is collapsible into a non-use position. Yet another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device which is light weight for minimizing weight carried in the field. And still another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device with sufficient versatility so that it may be used for most any type of bow. A further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a quick release lock to prevent unintended opening of the carrying loops. And a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device with detachable accessories for enabling the user to carry a bow carry all device. Yet a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device which includes a carrying case which can be stored in the archer's bow bag. And yet a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a combination stabilizing device and carrying case assembly that can be used without losing time while hunting for a bow which is stuck in the brush. A further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having an accessory which will hold accessory tools within reach while maintaining the accessory in a position which will not interfere with the user while hunting. And still another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a handle which can conveniently be gripped by the user when the device is used in an upright position. And still a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having an accessory which will enable the user to carry a bow when the device is not attached to the bow. And still a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having an accessory which will enable the user to carry and protect a bow while shooting at a target. A further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device which is light weight and easily folded into a non-use position without requiring disassembly. And a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a carrying case and integral stabilizing device mounted in conjunction with the carrying case to maintain a bow in a upright position while being transported. Still a further object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having a carrying case which can be folded in on itself for compact storage. Yet another object of the present invention is to provide a bow recovery device having an accessory which holds accessory tools in a convenient manner within reach while maintaining the accessory in a position which will not interfere with the user while hunting.
