Asteroid and Commet Speculative Mining Co., Inc. operates the E-Miners automated deep sea search platform for the discovery and extraction of metals and minerals from asteroids and comets. The company is also developing the E-Minerium® Blockchain platform for the mining of cryptocurrencies. SMTC operates the first and largest asteroid mining corporation Asteroid Digital Mining Co. (ADMC), which mines asteroids in the main belt of the solar system. For more information on SMTC, visit: www.smtec.tv For more information on ADMC, visit: www.asteroidmines.com About The International Asteroid Mining Conference The International Asteroid Mining Conference (IAMC) is a conference for mining enthusiasts, scientists, technologists, and business leaders. The conference will be a one-day event at the New World Center in Miami Beach, Florida. IAMC has become the leading event dedicated to asteroid and space mining. IAMC was created to share knowledge and information about asteroid mining, educate the public about this exciting endeavor, and explore the ways that this new and lucrative business will change the world. The event is expected to draw thousands of space enthusiasts, investors, miners and mining enthusiasts, space policy experts, representatives of leading technology and internet companies, financial experts, and members of the press. The 2018 IAMC was attended by several hundred people from all over the world. About Skycorp, Inc. Skycorp is focused on developing fully-autonomous robotic systems for in-space missions and future colonization of other worlds. We develop technology and space capabilities to enable humans to mine the asteroid belt and the moon. Skycorp’s technology for robotic mining is a critical component of future asteroid missions. Our current mining robot technology can mine ore from asteroids with multiple robotic spacecraft as components, using a system of robotic vessels that work together to accomplish a common goal. The first Skycorp mining craft have been designed and built and include autonomous hardware, software, robots and avionics. We have already created robotic systems that explore the surfaces of asteroids, map lunar craters, characterize and excavate surface regolith and other geological processes for the benefit of future missions and space colonization missions.
