This week in horror movies… “The Cabin in the Woods” This isn’t the first time someone has explored the zombie apocalypse with a group of college students who are also high school students. In fact, there have been plenty of zombie movies centered on a group of young adults like the ones in the series “Zombie High” which has been quite popular in the last few years. But, does “The Cabin in the Woods” have the edge over the others? The short answer is, no. While it’s no “Pineapple Express,” there is plenty of blood and gore and violence, some of which is gratuitous, even a little over the top. However, just like “The Cabin in the Woods” has its share of cliches, the other films that we’ve come to expect in this genre also have its share of flaws. Yet, despite the predictability of the film, “The Cabin in the Woods” is an enjoyable horror film that packs quite a punch for those who are in for a good scare. While it didn’t really do too well at the box office, it did earn a Best Horror Film from the jury at this year’s Fantastic Fest in Austin. In “The Cabin in the Woods,” five students volunteer to spend a week in an old cabin in the woods, only to discover it has secrets and more horrors than just zombies. The film is directed by Drew Goddard (known for films like “The Martian”).
