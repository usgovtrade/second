Concrete may have found it's killer app in graphene and more advanced composites, but the former materials are not as environmentally responsible as they claim. Graphene is the strongest material in existence (except for neutronium), and by itself is a wonder material that can be utilized in a variety of ways. For many, it looks like a combination of graphite and rubber, or graphite and plastic, but it's not. Graphite isn't a plastic, though, because it's a carbon-based material and is not composed of polymers. Graphene is composed of pure carbon atoms, a completely different form of matter. That means that graphene can't be welded or molded or compressed like polymers can. It can be made into very thin sheets, but it's still not plastic. That doesn't matter when it comes to composites, as you've probably heard. The idea is to place graphene sheets on the surface of polymers to give composites a unique set of properties. The graphene sheets provide enhanced toughness and an incredibly low coefficient of friction, which is how things stick together without wearing down. Namco Banzai 3 is a good example of such a composite. The game uses a new resin that combines both a plastic and a tough surface made of graphene. Composites aren't exactly new, but they have had limitations so far that prevent them from coming into their own as serious competitors to metals. Composites have always been much more expensive, which is why many composites are used in lightweight military vehicle applications. When you're only using a composite as an alternative to a metal, it needs to be pretty tough so that it doesn't fail. Graphene was supposed to make composites dramatically more competitive as materials, giving them an edge that hasn't been seen before.
